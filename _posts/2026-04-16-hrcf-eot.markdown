We show that <a href='https://coursefeedback.org/'>High-Resolution Course Feedback (HRCF)</a> can support measurable improvements in students’ perceived learning experience. Studying 103 HRCF course offerings of 42 computer science courses across 4 academic years, we find that each additional term of HRCF use is associated with modest but significant gains in learning-related evaluations for medium/low-enrollment courses (<250 students).
