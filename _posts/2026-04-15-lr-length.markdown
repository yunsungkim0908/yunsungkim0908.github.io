This paper revisits the <a href='https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2221311120'>claim</a> that students learn at surprisingly similar rates by testing how sensitive learning-rate estimates are to the length of students’ observed practice sequences. Across 26 datasets, truncating learning sequences at 10 or 5 opportunities substantially inflates estimated learning-rate heterogeneity, indicating that practice-sequence length is an important and underreported source of uncertainty in mixed-effects learning models.
