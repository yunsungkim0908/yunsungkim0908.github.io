Large language models show impressive performance on benchmarks. But do these results align with downstream tasks and their actual <i>intended impact</i>? Focusing on the task of teaching and learning of schoolchildren, we show that LLMs are poorly aligned with downstream measures of teaching quality and are often <i>negatively aligned</i> with the intended impact of student learning outcomes. This work demonstrates methods for robustly measuring alignment of complex tasks when the measurement of intended impact is noisy.
