This paper argues for a shift in how automated scoring should be formulated and studied: from treating automated scoring as an isolated prediction task to situating it within the broader stakeholder-centered assessment lifecycle spanning assessment design, scoring, and iterative refinement. We present four foundational, stakeholder-centered principles of interpretable automated scoring and demonstrate its feasibility by achieving high scoring accuracy and alignment with human judgment.
